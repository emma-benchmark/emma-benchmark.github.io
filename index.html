An Enhanced MultiModal ReAsoning Benchmark

Can MLLMs Reason/Think in Multimodality?

Overview of the EMMA dataset categories. Designed to evaluate multimodal reasoning across diverse domains.

Abstract

Visual reasoning is fundamental to human intelligence, yet the ability of Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) to perform true multimodal reasoning remains underexplored.

Existing benchmarks often emphasize shallow perceptual cues or text-dominant reasoning, leaving a gap in evaluating tasks that require integrated visual and textual reasoning.

We introduce EMMA (Enhanced MultiModal reAsoning), a benchmark targeting genuine multimodal reasoning across mathematics, physics, chemistry, and coding. EMMA tasks demand advanced visual manipulation and cross-modal reasoning, offering a rigorous evaluation of MLLM capabilities.

Evaluating state-of-the-art MLLMs on EMMA reveals significant limitations in handling complex multimodal reasoning and multi-hop tasks over image inputs, with even advanced techniques like chain-of-thought prompting underperforming. These findings underscore the need for improved multimodal architectures and training paradigms to close the gap between human and model reasoning in multimodal contexts.

EMMA sets a new standard for driving progress in this critical area.
